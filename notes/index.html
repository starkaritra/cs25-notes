Study Notes — Transformers United

Self-contained, visual, learn-by-understanding notes for the CS25 lectures. Read top to bottom: foundations first, then the architecture & interpretability deep-dives.

How to use these: read in order — each builds on the last. Don't just re-read: at each "✓ Check yourself" box, answer before expanding. End every note with the "Recap — rebuild it yourself" questions. Every note is print-to-PDF ready (Ctrl/Cmd-P → Save as PDF).

Batch 1 — Foundations

1

Transformers United — Overview (NLP · CV · RL)

~50 min · V1 L1

The map. Why RNNs failed (encoder bottleneck), how attention was born, the 2017 leap, and the "one architecture everywhere" convergence thesis.

 2

Introduction to Transformers — Andrej Karpathy

~1h15m · V2

The mechanics. Attention as message-passing, scaled dot-product attention, multi-head, positional encodings, the full block (residual + LayerNorm), causal masking.

 3

Transformers in Language — GPT

~50 min · V1 L2

Language modeling objective, BERT vs GPT, pretrain→adapt, the GPT scaling story, in-context / few-shot learning, scaling laws, emergent abilities.

 4

Transformers in Vision — ViT

~50 min · V1 L3

CNN inductive biases vs transformers, image→16×16 patches→tokens, [CLS] token, data-hungriness, attention maps, DETR & beyond.

 5

Decision Transformer — Reinforcement Learning

~50 min · V1 L4

RL as sequence modeling. Return-to-go conditioning ("upside-down RL"), offline RL, credit assignment via attention, benchmarks & limits.

Batch 2 — Architecture & Interpretability

6

Mixture of Experts & the Switch Transformer

~50 min · V1 L5

Scale params without scaling compute: sparse routing, top-1/top-k gating, load-balancing loss, expert parallelism, and the all-to-all comms bottleneck.

 7

Perceiver & Perceiver IO

~50 min · V1 L6

Break the O(N²) barrier: cross-attend a huge input onto a small latent array, then a cheap latent transformer. One backbone for any modality.

 8

Non-Parametric Transformers (NPTs)

~50 min · V1 L7

Attention *between datapoints*: take the whole dataset as input so predictions can depend on other points — learned, end-to-end, kNN-like reasoning.

 9

Transformer Circuits & Induction Heads

~50 min · V1 L8

Mechanistic interpretability: the residual stream, QK/OV circuits, head composition, and induction heads as the engine of in-context learning.

 10

Transformers for Audio, Speech & Music

~50 min · V1 L9

Taming very long signals: spectrogram patches, learned/discrete audio tokens, Wav2Vec/AST/Whisper, and relative attention for musical structure.

Batch 3 — Ideas, Alignment & Agents (CS25 V2)

V2's "Introduction to Transformers" (Karpathy) is already covered as Note 2.

11

Part-Whole Hierarchies — GLOM (Hinton)

~50 min · V2

Hinton's idea system: represent a per-image parse tree via "islands of agreement" across columns × levels, unifying transformers, neural fields, contrastive learning & capsules.

 12

Language & Human Alignment

~50 min · V2

The alignment gap and the RLHF recipe: SFT → reward model from preferences → PPO with a KL leash. Reward hacking, sycophancy, and the alignment tax.

 13

Emergent Abilities & Scaling

~50 min · V2

Smooth power-law scaling laws (Chinchilla) vs sharp "emergent" jumps — and the mirage argument that emergence can be an artifact of harsh metrics. Chain-of-thought.

 14

Strategic Games

~50 min · V2

Strategic reasoning under uncertainty: Nash equilibria, self-play + search (AlphaZero/MuZero), regret minimization for poker, and language+strategy (Diplomacy/CICERO).

 15

Robotics & Imitation Learning

~50 min · V2

Learning policies from demonstrations: behavior cloning, the compounding-error problem & DAgger, and transformer policies (RT-1/2, Gato) for language-conditioned control.

Batch 4 — Reasoning, Science & Embodied Agents (CS25 V2→V3)

16

Common-Sense Reasoning

~50 min · V2

The "dark matter" of intelligence: reporting bias, ATOMIC/COMET commonsense knowledge graphs, symbolic knowledge distillation, and where LLMs still break.

 17

Biomedical Transformers

~50 min · V2

Biology as language: protein LMs (ESM), AlphaFold's Evoformer, genomics (DNA LMs), and clinical/medical LLMs (BioBERT, Med-PaLM) — plus the data & privacy challenges.

 18

Neuroscience-Inspired AI

~50 min · V2

Brain ↔ AI: modern Hopfield networks as attention, predictive coding, hippocampus/grid cells and transformers — and where the "attention" analogy breaks.

 19

Low-level Embodied Intelligence

~50 min · V3

Bridging semantic knowledge to motor control: SayCan affordances, Code-as-Policies, and Vision-Language-Action models (PaLM-E, RT-1/2) that emit action tokens.

 20

Generalist Agents in Open Worlds

~50 min · V3

Open-ended learning in Minecraft: MineDojo + MineCLIP's learned reward, Voyager's LLM-written skill library & self-curriculum, and VPT's "GPT for actions".

Batch 5 — Assistants, Translation & Retrieval (CS25 V3)

21

How I Learned to Love the Transformer

~50 min · V3

A deep intuition/mental-model talk on why transformers work — the core lens for reasoning about attention, scale, and the architecture's generality.

 22

Recipe for Training Helpful Chatbots

~50 min · V3

The hands-on assistant recipe: pretrain → instruction/SFT → preference data → reward model → RLHF (PPO) / DPO, plus data, evaluation (Arena/Elo) and failure modes.

 23

No Language Left Behind

~50 min · V3

Meta's 200-language translation: bitext mining via sentence embeddings, a Mixture-of-Experts multilingual model, FLORES-200 eval, and the low-resource challenge.

 24

Beyond LLMs: Agents & Guided Reasoning

~50 min · V3

Four frontiers: LLM agents (ReAct/tool use), emergent abilities, intermediate-guided reasoning (CoT, self-consistency, tree-of-thoughts), and the BabyLM sample-efficiency push.

 25

Retrieval-Augmented Language Models

~50 min · V3

Give the model an open book: dense retrieval (DPR), RAG/Fusion-in-Decoder/RETRO/Atlas, parametric vs non-parametric knowledge, and why retrieval curbs hallucination.

Batch 6 — The 2024 Frontier (CS25 V4)

26

Overview of Transformers (V4, 2024)

~1 hr · V4

A refreshed intro: tight mechanics recap plus the 2024 landscape — decoder-only dominance, scaling & emergence, multimodality, long context, and efficiency (FlashAttention, MoE).

 27

Jason Wei & Hyung Won Chung (OpenAI)

~1 hr · V4

Intuitions, not architecture: next-token prediction as multi-task learning, emergence & chain-of-thought, the bitter lesson, encoder-decoder→decoder-only, "don't teach, incentivize".

 28

Aligning Open Language Models

~1 hr · V4

The open-alignment timeline & recipe: Alpaca/Vicuna/Zephyr/Tülu, RLHF vs DPO, preference & synthetic data, and evaluation culture (Arena/Elo, MT-Bench).

 29

Demystifying Mixtral of Experts

~50 min · V4

A leading open sparse MoE: 8 experts, top-2 routing, ~47B params but ~13B active per token; routing behavior, the memory-vs-compute tradeoff. (Builds on Note 6.)

 30

Near-Shallow Architectures

~50 min · V4

How shallow can a transformer go? Trading depth for better per-layer transforms — expressivity vs depth, iterative refinement, and the efficiency payoff.

 32

Behind the Scenes of LLM Pre-training (StarCoder)

 33

Hyung Won Chung (OpenAI): Scaling & the Future

Research in the age of the compute exponential: the bitter lesson, assumptions with a shelf life, removing structure over time, and "don't teach, incentivize".

 34

Overview of Transformers (V5, 2025)

The 2025 frontier: reasoning models & test-time compute (o1/R1), RL for reasoning with verifiable rewards, agents, native multimodality, and attention alternatives.

 35

RL as a Co-Design of Product & Research

Karina Nguyen on the loop where post-training, product, and research feed each other: RL shapes model behavior/character, and product feedback drives the data flywheel.

Batch 8 — Reasoning, Interpretability & Generation (CS25 V5)

36

The Advent of AGI

~50 min · V5

Div Garg on the path from chatbots to autonomous agents: the perceive→plan→act loop, computer/web agents, long-horizon reliability, and the compounding-error problem.

 37

Large Language Model Reasoning

~50 min · V5

Denny Zhou (DeepMind): LLMs can reason if you elicit intermediate steps — chain-of-thought, CoT-decoding, self-consistency, and least-to-most as serial test-time compute.

 38

On the Biology of a Large Language Model

~50 min · V5

Josh Batson (Anthropic): reverse-engineering models like organisms — superposition, sparse-autoencoder features, and attribution graphs for planning, arithmetic & refusals.

 39

Multimodal World Models for Drug Discovery

~50 min · V5

Eshed Margalit (Noetik.ai): fusing spatial omics, histology & perturbation screens into a world model that simulates cell responses for in-silico drug discovery.

 40

Transformers in Diffusion Models

~50 min · V5

Diffusion in a nutshell, then the Diffusion Transformer (DiT): patchify the noisy latent, denoise with a transformer + adaLN conditioning — the engine behind SD3 & Sora.

From Representation Learning to World Modeling

Next-Token Prediction → Next-Generation Intelligence

Collaborative AI Agents for Science & Medicine
